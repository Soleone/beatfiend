# Research: Preliminary naming clearance for rhythm-game and beatmap-editor candidates

## Summary

None of the eight candidates looks clean enough to adopt without changing course. **BeatForge, SongForge, BeatLab, SongLab, and JamForge are the most direct collisions**, because exact or effectively exact names are already used for music creation, rhythm, or kid-oriented music software. **TuneLab, HitLab, and PlayLab also carry high risk** through established adjacent music/software uses, a child-entertainment filing, or crowded exact-name use.

This is a preliminary public-web screen, not a comprehensive trademark search and not legal advice. A trademark lawyer should confirm live status, ownership, priority, related goods, state and international records, and common-law rights before launch.

### Cross-cutting conclusions

1. **The “music word + Lab/Forge” field is crowded.** Every candidate produced exact public use, and seven produced exact music, software, game, or child-entertainment use. The suffix variation does not create a meaningful clean lane on this evidence.
2. **Same-market collision is strongest for SongForge and BeatForge.** SongForge describes essentially a kid-oriented DAW/game-music tool, while BeatForge is active beat-production and sequencing software. These are more probative than merely unrelated trademark hits. [SongForge](https://songforge.makegl.com/about.html) [BeatForge](https://beatforge.nl/)
3. **App-store uniqueness is already lost for several names.** BeatLab, SongLab, and TuneLab have exact Apple music-app listings. PlayLab has a child education app, and Beat Forge surfaced as a rhythm app, subject to territory/status verification.
4. **Domain practicality is poor.** Exact branded domains are already visibly in use for `beatlab.app`, `hitlab.com`, `jamforge.org`, `beatforge.nl`, `tunelab-world.com`, and `playlab.ai`; SongForge has an active branded subdomain. This screen did not run registrar/RDAP checks for the bare `.com` of every candidate, so availability, ownership, and aftermarket price remain unverified.
5. **Trademark-index results are leads, not final status opinions.** Third-party indexes can lag. The exact SONGLAB and PLAYLAB applications should be refreshed in [USPTO Trademark Search](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search/search) and [TSDR](https://tsdr.uspto.gov/), including Class 9 game/downloadable software, Class 41 entertainment, and Class 42 SaaS, plus spacing and phonetic variants.

### Candidate worth deeper legal clearance

**No candidate earns a positive clearance recommendation from this set.** If a stakeholder insists on preserving one, **PlayLab** is the least directly occupied in rhythm-game or beatmap-editor software, but it still has an exact child entertainment/music application and active education software/app uses. A narrow lawyer-led knockout search could determine whether those rights are fatal, but a fresh, more distinctive name is likely more efficient.

### Dropped or down-weighted

- Generic name-generator, SEO, social-media, artist-name, and unrelated product results - Too weak to assess source confusion for the proposed goods.
- Healthcare HITLAB - Retained only as crowding context, not treated as a same-market blocker.
- BEATLAB ACADEMY abandoned filing and older abandoned music HITLAB filing - Not treated as live federal rights, only evidence of naming history and possible common-law use.
- `songforge.org` - Search surfaced it, but DNS resolution failed during direct fetch, so it was not relied on as current product evidence.
- Apple “Beat Forge” rhythm listing - Search surfaced it, but the page returned not found on follow-up, so it was treated as a verification lead rather than decisive evidence.

## Gaps and residual risks

- No attorney-supervised comprehensive search was performed across USPTO live/dead records, state registries, WIPO/Madrid, EUIPO, UKIPO, Canada, or other launch markets.
- Exact status and prosecution history for indexed filings must be checked in TSDR by serial number. Similarity searches should include spaces, plurals, phonetics, design marks, owners, and related goods.
- Common-law priority, actual geographic reach, sales, reputation, coexistence agreements, and litigation/opposition history were not established.
- Bare-domain availability was not verified by RDAP/registrar for every TLD, and a domain being available would not establish trademark clearance.
- App-store availability varies by country and time. Google Play coverage was less complete than Apple coverage.
- A distinctive new coined mark should undergo the same legal clearance before investment.
