## 25 name candidates

These are creative naming suggestions only. Trademark, domain, app-store, and company-name availability were not checked.

### 1. Making and crafting

1. **Beatloom**: Beatmaps woven from musical threads.
2. **GrooveSketch**: Captures the draft, jam, and refine workflow.
3. **NoteWeaver**: Creative and approachable, with a musical focus.
4. **SoundStitch**: Suggests placing musical events into a cohesive pattern.
5. **Loopwright**: A distinctive maker-oriented name with more sophistication than “Loop Maker.”

### 2. Mapping and structure

6. **Pulseplot**: Plotting rhythmic events onto a timeline.
7. **Jamgrid**: Directly connects live input with beat-grid editing.
8. **TempoCanvas**: Music becomes a surface for creative construction.
9. **Rhythm Atlas**: Suggests mapping, exploration, and multiple musical worlds.
10. **Gridgroove**: Combines DAW structure with rhythmic feel.

### 3. Motion and energy

11. **Grooveglide**: Smooth, musical, and kinetic.
12. **BeatSpring**: Buoyant energy without sounding aggressive.
13. **TempoTrail**: Notes form a playable path through a song.
14. **Soundstride**: Confident forward motion with broad musical appeal.
15. **NoteFlux**: Notes in motion, suitable for both editing and playtesting.

### 4. Play and exploration

16. **GrooveGarden**: Beatmaps grow through experimentation and refinement.
17. **Beat Harbor**: A friendly home for importing, shaping, and playing music.
18. **TempoQuest**: Playful and goal-oriented without skewing too young.
19. **JamOrbit**: Musical ideas circle the grid and return as gameplay.
20. **Rhythm Roam**: Invites exploration across songs and patterns.

### 5. Distinctive and coined

21. **Melodrift**: Melody plus motion, atmospheric but still approachable.
22. **Jammetry**: Jam plus geometry, reflecting live performance on a structured grid.
23. **Rhythora**: A brand-like construction derived from rhythm.
24. **Temvora**: A compact coined name with tempo and motion associations.
25. **Sonivelle**: A softer, polished sound-oriented name.

## Top 8, briefly explained

1. **Beatloom**  
   The strongest metaphor for the complete product. Players weave imported music, grid timing, recorded input, and refinement into a playable map.

2. **Pulseplot**  
   Compact and memorable. “Plot” communicates both timeline placement and constructing a playable journey.

3. **GrooveSketch**  
   Particularly well aligned with automatic generation as a draft. It makes experimentation feel intentional rather than unfinished.

4. **Jammetry**  
   Distinctive and conceptually precise: spontaneous jamming meets beat-grid geometry. The unusual spelling may need light pronunciation guidance.

5. **Melodrift**  
   Expressive, kinetic, and more expansive than a drum-only name. It could support a visually rich game identity.

6. **Rhythm Atlas**  
   Frames each beatmap as a musical world to chart and explore. Slightly less compact, but rich in visual and product-language possibilities.

7. **Loopwright**  
   Positions the user as a skilled musical maker. It feels creative and capable without becoming overly technical.

8. **JamOrbit**  
   Friendly, energetic, and visually suggestive. It connects repeated musical cycles with movement and play.

## Recommended top 5

1. **Beatloom**
2. **Pulseplot**
3. **GrooveSketch**
4. **Jammetry**
5. **Melodrift**

**Best overall:** **Beatloom**  
**Best for clarity:** **Pulseplot**  
**Best for a friendly creative identity:** **GrooveSketch**  
**Most distinctive concept:** **Jammetry**  
**Best for a kinetic game-forward identity:** **Melodrift**

## Review findings

- **No blockers:** The top candidates fit the requested musical, kinetic, creative, and age-flexible tone.
- **Medium:** Trademark, domain, app-store, social-handle, and international-language screening remains necessary for every candidate.
- **Low:** More descriptive options such as `TempoCanvas`, `Jamgrid`, and `Gridgroove` may be harder to distinguish than the coined or metaphorical names.
- **Low:** `Jammetry`, `Rhythora`, `Temvora`, and `Sonivelle` may require pronunciation testing.
- File-path findings are not applicable because this was a naming review and no project files were reviewed or edited.
